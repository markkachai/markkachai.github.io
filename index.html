Citi Bike Case Study

The objective of this final group project was to develop a tool to find the number of bikes to stock in a selection of stations at the beginning of the day to maximize the number of daily bike trips. Applying the 4-step analytics process to solve this problem, our group constructed a data-driven narrative prepared in a pitch presentation for the Citi Bike executives. In RStudio, descriptive analyses helped to identify demand patterns, and predictive analyses were done through the use of a multiple linear regression model created to forecast daytime and evening demand for five stations. An optimization model created in Excel was used to find the optimal initial allocation of bikes as well as the number of trips.

COVID-19 Data Exploration and Visualization Project

The objective of this project was to visualize key COVID-19 findings in the form of dashboards in Tableau. The data used was a collection of COVID-19 data maintained by Our World in Data. Data cleaning and preparation were done in Excel. This file was then uploaded to a database and table created in MySQL, where the dataset was analyzed to find key COVID-19 metrics. Certain tables queried in MySQL were uploaded to Excel, and then into Tableau. Two dashboards were created, which covered many key data points of the COVID-19 pandemic.

Super Bowl Data Analysis Project

In this project, I examined the correlation between Super Bowl ad spending and generated social media buzz to determine whether investing millions in such ads leads to increased attention online. Assuming the role of a business analyst recently hired for an analytics project at a major firm that was deciding to launch a new car the following year, I was tasked with figuring out whether or not the company should spend on advertising a new car launch around the Super Bowl the following year. I used RStudio to explore, analyze, and visualize the data and support my recommendations.
